  • Khabib Abdulmanapovich Nurmagomedov

    Russian former mixed martial artist
    also known as The Eagle

    Khabib Abdulmanapovich Nurmagomedov (born 20 September 1988) is a Russian former professional mixed martial artist who competed in the Lightweight division of the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC). He was the longest-reigning UFC Lightweight Champion ever, having held the title from April 2018 to March 2021. With 29 wins and no losses, he retired with an undefeated record.[8] Nurmagomedov is widely considered to be among the greatest mixed martial artists of all time,[9][10][11][12][13] and was inducted into the UFC Hall of Fame on June 30, 2022.[14]

    Why did Khabib Nurmagomedov retire?
    Khabib announced his retirement from MMA after his UFC 254 victory against Justin Gaethje in October 2020. The primary reason cited was his promise to his mother and family, and the emotional toll of the passing of his father and coach, Abdulmanap Nurmagomedov.

    Khabib retired with an undefeated record of 29-0 in professional MMA, making him one of the few fighters in the sport's history to retire without a loss.

    Khabib is known for his relentless grappling and wrestling skills. His training regimen includes a focus on conditioning, wrestling, and various forms of martial arts to complement his well-rounded skill set.
    Who are some of Khabib's notable opponents in the UFC?
    Notable opponents include Conor McGregor, Dustin Poirier, Justin Gaethje, and Rafael dos Anjos. His victory over McGregor in UFC 229 became one of the most-watched and talked-about fights in MMA history.

  • Early Life & Career

    Khabib Abdulmanapovich Nurmagomedov was born to an Avar family on 20 September 1988 in the village of Sildi in the Tsumadinsky District of the Dagestan ASSR, an autonomous republic within the Russian SFSR, Soviet Union.[22][23][24] He has an older brother, Magomed, and younger sister, Amina.[25] His father's family had moved from Sildi to Kirovaul, where his father converted the ground floor of their two-story building into a gym. Nurmagomedov grew up in the household with his siblings and cousins.[25] His interest in martial arts began when watching students training at the gym.[26][27] Khabib's training as a child included wrestling a bear when he was nine years old.
